Ph.D. Student in Physics

Yifan Liu

Institute for Solid State Physics, The University of Tokyo

My research explores universal phenomena in quantum many-body systems through the lens of (boundary) conformal field theory. I combine analytical field-theoretic methods with tensor-network simulations and entanglement-based diagnostics to bridge microscopic lattice models and their continuum descriptions. More broadly, I am interested in using quantum field theory to understand critical phenomena in lattice systems, with particular interests in boundary criticality, quantum-information measures of many-body states, and, more recently, non-Hermitian physics.

Research Interests

More research details
  • Field-theoretic descriptions of quantum critical phenomena in lattice systems.
  • Boundary conformal field theory, boundary criticality, and boundary renormalization-group flows.
  • Quantum-information measures and entanglement in many-body states, explored with tensor-network methods.
  • More recently, non-Hermitian physics and nonunitary critical phenomena.
