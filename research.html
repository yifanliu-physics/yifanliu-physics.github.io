Research

Field theory and quantum criticality in lattice systems

My research uses conformal and quantum field theory, together with tensor-network methods, to understand universal phenomena in quantum many-body systems. My work centers on boundary criticality, quantum-information measures of many-body states, and universal phenomena and dynamics in non-Hermitian systems.

Research Themes

Boundary CFT and quantum criticality

I use boundary conformal field theory, conformal perturbation theory, and DMRG to study boundary conditions and boundary renormalization-group flows in microscopic quantum chains. This includes finite-size corrections in gapless one-dimensional systems and boundary criticality in the quantum Ashkin-Teller model. I am also exploring broader applications of boundary field-theoretic ideas to critical systems with modulated structures.

Tensor networks and quantum information

I use matrix product states, finite-entanglement scaling, and entanglement-based diagnostics to understand how universal continuum behavior emerges from microscopic many-body states and finite-bond-dimension approximations. Current work examines connections between generalized quantum measurements and boundary phenomena in critical systems.

Non-Hermitian critical phenomena and dynamics

More recently, I have been exploring non-Hermitian critical chains and nonunitary field theories, including ways to extract boundary conformal data from periodic microscopic models. I am also studying universal dynamics near non-Hermitian critical points.
